Curriculum Overview

The curriculum is designed to provide a broad and balanced education to all students.
It is based on the National Curriculum of England and Wales, modified to meet the needs of our international student body.
The curriculum is divided into three stages: Early Years, Primary and Secondary.
The Early Years stage is for children aged 3 to 5 years.
The Primary stage is for children aged 5 to 11 years.
The Secondary stage is for students aged 11 to 16 years.
The curriculum is delivered in English, with an emphasis on the development of high standards of literacy and numeracy.
The curriculum is enriched by a wide range of extra-curricular activities, including sports, performing arts, music, art and design, and community service.

Early Years

The Early Years stage is for children aged 3 to 5 years.
The curriculum is based on the Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) framework, which is used in England and Wales.
The EYFS framework sets out seven areas of learning and development.
All areas of learning and development are important and inter-connected.
Three areas are particularly crucial for igniting childrenâ€™s curiosity and enthusiasm for learning, and for building their capacity to learn,
form relationships and thrive. These three areas, the prime areas, are:


  • Communication and language
  • Physical development
  • Personal, social and emotional development

We also support children in four specific areas, through which the three prime areas are strengthened and applied.
The specific areas are:


  • Literacy
  • Mathematics
  • Understanding the world
  • Expressive arts and design

The curriculum is delivered through a combination of teacher-directed activities and child-initiated play.
The curriculum is delivered in English, with an emphasis on the development of high standards of literacy and numeracy.

Primary

The Primary stage is for children aged 5 to 11 years.
The curriculum is based on the National Curriculum of England and Wales, modified to meet the needs of our international student body.
The curriculum is divided into two key stages: Key Stage 1 (Years 1 and 2) and Key Stage 2 (Years 3 to 6).
The curriculum is delivered in English, with an emphasis on the development of high standards of literacy and numeracy.
The curriculum is enriched by a wide range of extra-curricular activities, including sports, performing arts, music, art and design, and community service.

Secondary

The Secondary stage is for students aged 11 to 16 years.
The curriculum is based on the National Curriculum of England and Wales, modified to meet the needs of our international student body.
The curriculum is divided into two key stages: Key Stage 3 (Years 7 to 9) and Key Stage 4 (Years 10 and 11).
